## The Starting Point
I interned at Graviton Research Capital LLP as a quantitative researcher after my sixth semester which was the semester where I was exposed to Reinforcement Learning and got truly excited by it. After my internship I started having doubts about what I want to do with my life going forward? Should I continue working as a quantitative researcher at Graviton and keep earning money by doing financial analysis and develop trading strategies based on the insights. On the other hand should I be working on solving some fundamental problems which are lying at the frontier of the current technological developments.

This confusion has been there for a long time and to a very large extent cause I always wanted to build something that is much more meaningful than just making money for the company I am working or just for myself. Along with this I was not sure how to get to frontier research labs without any PhD but for a PhD in the field of AI/ML I didn't have any strong research work done in that area. During this confusion I saw a mail from the SENAI Labs at IITM who are taking students to work in the area of Data Science. So I applied for that position and got into it, this is where I realised there are lot more interesting problems than trying to crack the markets where the final output mostly benefits only you or the company you are working for rather than something big coming out of it. This is when I realised this is something which I want to do so my confusion about what to do going forward was gone. Then came the real question how to do it? I was looking for research positions if there any openings in any of the labs which are working in the field of AI/ML where I found OpenAI Residency Program, Apple ML Residency Program, Neel Nanda's MATS Program and Google DeepMind Pre-Doc Researcher Position.

After looking at all of these opportunities there was a relief that the question of how to get to frontier is addressed to some extent giving me some leads where to go. I applied to all of these with Deepmind being the favourite (this started from the RL course under Prof. Balaraman Ravindran where I got first introduced to the techniques used by GDM to create AlphaGo and I was truly amazed).

## The Application Process

I filled out the primary application on google careers portal with all the necessary details. I was waiting for the result and I was quite doubtful as I remember seeing that this position is for Master's students and me being an undergrad I was not sure whether there would be a chance for me. The main reason for me applying for this position despite of the confusion is the following: `If, I don't apply then there is definitely not a possibility otherwise the worst case possibility is still the same. But, the best case is something that would change my trajectory completely and positively for me.` (also I was confident that if they see my application I have a good chance for getting selected). After this I started spending my winter roaming around and travelling to various places.

When I was on a road I received a mail from the hiring manager of Google asking me to fill a few more details that are necessary for the role and this kind of gave me the confidence they are considering me and I am going to get an interview opportunity. This made me really happy so I decided to fill the form but I was in the middle of a road trip and in the happiness it slipped out of my mind and I remembered it suddenly when I was taking a bath under a waterfall on Jan 5th at around 3:30 PM near Kanyakumari and the deadline was at 5:00 PM the same day (please don't think I was not serious, I was really serious about the role but somehow such a thing happened). I was panicked at that moment what would I do in such a short notice and I am supposed to write my statement of purpose and few other details. I came running out of the waterfall and I just dressed up very fastly and took my laptop sat in a nearby hotel and started filling the form and polishing it and the sheer amount of stress during that one hour is inexplicable. Finally, I managed to submit it by 4:59 PM, uffff. 

Only suggestion is that try not to push the situation to this edge and you might lose on the opportunities. I think for me this is a side-effect of my philisophy of maximising my experiences and live every single moment to the extreme so I try to push things to the last possible minute and this time it happened unknowingly.

## The Interview Process
Once the form is filled after a few weeks I got a scheduling mail from the HR saying that there will be 2 rounds, one coding round and one research round. The interview timings would be a bit flexible and they are willing to slightly reschedule based on your availability so I mentioned what timings would work for me and then they scheduled accordingly. 

### Research Round
In this round I was given a paper to read approximately 2 days before the interview and I am expected to understand the methods that were mentioned in the paper. I would roughly summarize the expectations of the interview as follows:
- What is the problem the paper is trying to solve?
- What are the techniques the paper came up with?
- What are immediate improvements/extensions of the paper?
- What are the critical issues with the paper? How would you address them?
- Along with this how comfortable are you with the basic fundamentals behind the paper or roughly what you mentioned as the research interests.

Apart from this the interviewer [Dr. Karthikeyan Shanmugam](https://sites.google.com/view/karthikeyan-shanmugam/) asked me about the projects I did and asked why I made a few choices and what was the reasoning behind them. I was able to answer most of the questions and I stumbled a bit in the beginning but after that the interview went pretty good in my opinion. I would say the following few things helped in my research round, being clear about what I did before and having a clear idea of the projects I did and eye for detail for the choices I have made. For the research paper part I was lucky enough to coincidentally focusing my preparation along the same lines as I mentioned above and after completing the paper I also went through the reviews that were given on the paper and this quickly gave me what are the immediate issues (perceived issues) with the paper and I had some starting points of what are the questions that are to be immediately addressed. Personally, I would say my interview was 7-8/10.

### Coding Round
For me personally this is the place where I was not very confident in the beginning. Since, it was very long back since I coded something and I was not sure whether I will be able to solve any of the problems then I started practising the DSA problems from leet code this is when I became a bit more confident that I didn't forget a lot of concepts and I am still able to write good code. The interview went pretty great and I was able to solve both the problems that were asked and a little bit of discussion about how I would identify the corner cases what are the tests I would perform inorder to ensure correctness of the code. So I would say this was a 7-8/10 as well.

## The Post Interview Discussions and Expectations

After approximately 2-weeks after the coding round I got a mail from [Dr. Praneeth Netrapalli](https://praneethnetrapalli.org/) asking whether I would be available for a 30-min chat. This is the moment of relief for me that said I cleared the technical tests and they would like to understand how good researcher could I become. So we scheduled and met over a google meet where we discussed about how I ended up applying and what was my trajectory during my last 4 years here at IIT Madras and discussion about the projects I did and the motivation behind the choice of the problem and the subtle details of the details and also whether I was able to conduct successful experiments and analyse the results properly and proceed towards the next steps based on the inferences. Personally I felt it was a 10/10 because it was mostly a good discussion and it felt like more of a discussion to understand me rather than test me, this also gave me a feeling if I join Google as a pre-doc researcher then I would be having similar discussions rather than just a helper to conduct experiments and it would be mostly a mentor-collaborator vibe.

After this I got a mail from [Dr. Prateek Jain](https://www.prateekjain.org/) for an informal chat this is the point where I was confident that I am going to get an offer. In this call Prateek asked me a few questions about what I am expecting from the role and what are my future plans and what are the things that excite me the most in research. Later, he explained me what are the expectations from me and what are the expectations I could have and what are the possible paths that I can pursue after the pre-doc position. This is something I felt very nice about cause it is very important for me to know clearly what I am getting into before making a choice. After this call I made my mind and decision.

## Why this decision?

I think if you are reading this, then may this section might keep you at ease if you are trying to understand why I took this particular decision.

- Why are you declining a Quant offer for something that is very less paying?
> I understand that the money that I might be getting is less compared to the quant offer I had with me. I believe that before settling for the quant offer it is really important for me to understand what I want to do for the rest of my life. As of now, I think I am not very sure that I want to be a quant researcher for the rest of my life (this might change in the future but for now it is not the case). And apart from this I feel I have a few questions in my mind which I would like to see solutions for and work towards them and GDM feels like the right place for me right now and the best possible choice for me for the path of life I want to travel along.

- Are you saying money is not important for you at all?
> No, I understand and respect money and in my opinion money is important and in some sense shows how good you are doing what you are doing. But more than just the output for me how I earn the money is also important and what I am going to do for 8-10 hours everyday for the rest of my life is much more important than the money. Also looking into the big-picture there might not be pretty big differences in the earnings of the either paths if I dedicate myself completely to it, which I will try my best to do.

- Why not work for one or two years in Quant and then go for what you want to do?
> There are many reasons for this but I would like to highlight a few of them:
> - The pace of the current AI/ML industry is so high that the problems I feel important right now would be still open by then. I don't think you can keep up with the current advancements in this field along with a high-intensity Quant job.
> - I can always come back stronger to the Quant industry if I take this role but I don't think the other way around will be smooth enough and I might have to work much more harder and might get burnt out in the process which I don't see healthy.
> - The money factor, I think once you start earning large amounts there would be a natural comfort which gets formed which makes to unsee what I feel important right now. This is something which I don't want to do. I want to give a genuine attempt at the problems which I feel are important before someone solves them and then me regretting that I should have made an attempt to solve them.

- Do you think you will regret this decision?
> I don't think so. If I think so why would I take it? Also I strongly believe in taking ownership for my decisions and actions rather than listening for advices and getting influenced and this seems the most rewarding step I can take right now.
